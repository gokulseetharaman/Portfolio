About Me

I'm Gokul Seetharaman — a hands-on engineer passionate about building intelligent systems at the intersection of artificial intelligence, robotics, and automation. With a solid foundation in Electrical and Electronics Engineering and currently pursuing my MSc in Robotics and Autonomous Systems at Aston University, I bring a unique blend of hardware proficiency and software expertise.

My focus lies in developing real-world solutions that combine machine intelligence with physical systems. I work with tools like PyTorch, OpenCV, ROS2, and embedded platforms to design systems that sense, learn, and act. I'm currently developing several projects, including a voice-controlled robotic arm, a gesture-based virtual drawing board, and lightweight language models optimized for edge deployment.

Curiosity and real-world impact are what drive me. I thrive on solving problems that matter — whether it's improving automation, enhancing human-machine collaboration, or pushing the boundaries of what intelligent systems can do.

Outside of engineering, I’m actively involved in open-source communities and passionate about tech for good — especially in areas like sustainability, ethical AI, and robotics for social impact.
